About

I build large-scale empirical studies that link climate and economic shocks to human-capital outcomes — births, child health, learning, and labor mobility — using administrative microdata across the U.S. and Mexico. Before my Ph.D., I led a multi-country research program at the Association of Supervisors of Banks of the Americas in Mexico City, and was a research fellow at the Bank for International Settlements in Basel. I care about reproducibility, and I orchestrate LLM agents across my research pipelines.
